How much does it cost to build a pool in Miami?

Andres Garcia
Project Manager

Every splash counts 

Dreaming of making a splash with your very own pool in the sunny backdrop of Miami? You're not alone. With its balmy weather and vibrant lifestyle, Miami is the perfect place to have a private oasis right in your backyard. But before you dive headfirst into pool ownership, it's crucial to understand what goes into the cost of building a pool. From the ground up, we'll explore permits, materials, labor, and all the essential aspects that add up to your pool-building budget. 

Permits and regulations 

Before any construction begins, you'll need to obtain the necessary permits from local authorities. In Miami, the cost of a pool permit can vary widely depending on your specific location and the scope of your project. On average, permit fees cost approximately 2% of the construction cost. Remember, this is a critical step to ensure your pool meets all local zoning and safety regulations, so don't skip it! 

Design and size 

The design and size of your pool are where your dreams meet reality. Want a simple rectangular pool? Or are you leaning towards a more elaborate lagoon-style oasis with waterfalls? The complexity of your design will significantly affect the cost. A basic pool installation in Miami can start at around $50,000, but for those looking for more luxurious designs, the sky's (or your budget's) the limit, with costs easily soaring to $100,000 or more. 

Materials

The materials you choose for your pool will impact both its appearance and cost. The three main options for a concrete pool finish are plaster, aggregates or tile. For many, cost is the overriding factor in determining the type of interior surface. 

Labor

Labor costs for building a pool in Miami can vary based on the complexity of the project and the reputation of the contractor. Typically, labor can account for about half of the total cost of the pool. It's essential to get multiple quotes and read reviews to find a reliable contractor who can bring your vision to life within your budget. 

Additional features 

Thinking about adding some extra flair to your pool with lighting, a smart pool system, heating system, or perhaps a jacuzzi? Each additional feature can significantly increase your overall costs. For example, installing a heating system can add around $5,000 to your budget, while fancy lighting or a spa addition can increase your costs by several thousand dollars.

Maintenance and upkeep 

Don't forget about the ongoing costs of pool ownership. Regular maintenance, including cleaning, chemical balancing, and equipment checks, can run you about $2,500 annually in Miami. Investing in a pool cover and efficient equipment can help reduce these costs over time. Here are some tips on pool maintenance:

  • Weekly Maintenance 
  • Vacuum the bottom and sides 
  • Test and adjust 
  • Test the water level and adjust as needed 
  • Clean coping around the water surface 
  • Use algae removal products as needed 
  • Shock the pool as instructed on the product label 

Timeline

A pool project in Miami involves two key phases: obtaining plans and permits and then moving on to construction. Initially, designing your pool and securing permits can take 2-3 months, a crucial period for ensuring everything is up to code. Following that, the construction phase typically spans 3 to 6 months, depending on the project's complexity and external factors like weather. Altogether, from the first sketch to your inaugural swim, the entire process usually unfolds over 4 to 8 months.
