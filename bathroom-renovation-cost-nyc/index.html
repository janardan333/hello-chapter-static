How much
does a bathroom renovation cost
in NYC?

Sebastian Aguas
VP Construction

There is never just one specific answer or range to determine a bathroom renovation cost, it’s always a case-to-case basis for every renovation project. However, you can get an estimated cost by basing it on your situation and budget, plus these 3 important factors we’re sharing that’ll make up just about how much you might need for your dream bathroom.

Bathroom Renovation, Tribeca, NYC

1. Bathroom size

The size of your bathroom is a key factor in determining renovation costs. Smaller bathrooms, typically 25 to 35 square feet, generally require less material and labor, resulting in lower expenses. In contrast, larger bathrooms exceeding 100 square feet may involve more extensive renovations and incur higher costs.

2. Scope of work

The scope of your bathroom renovation project significantly affects the total cost. Basic cosmetic updates like fixtures, tiles, and paint replacements are cheaper compared to extensive overhauls involving structural or layout changes.

Common elements included in the scope of work for a bathroom renovation include:

  • Demolition of existing fixtures and finishes
  • Plumbing and electrical updates or rerouting
  • Installation of new fixtures such as toilets, sinks, showers, and bathtubs
  • Flooring replacement or refinishing
  • Tile work for walls, floors, and shower surrounds
  • Vanity and cabinetry installation
  • Lighting fixtures and ventilation upgrades
  • Paint or wallpaper application

The complexity of each task and the extent of desired customization will impact the overall cost of your bathroom renovation project.

Bathroom Remodel, Hamptons, NY

3. Materials and finishes

The materials chosen for your bathroom renovation play a pivotal role in determining costs. With a diverse range available, from economic options to luxurious finishes, there's something to match every preference and budget.

Here are some key material considerations and their associated costs:

  • Flooring: Options include ceramic or porcelain tile, vinyl, laminate, hardwood, or natural stone. Prices vary based on the material chosen, with ceramic tile being more affordable compared to marble or hardwood.
  • Fixtures: The cost of fixtures such as toilets, sinks, faucets, showers, and bathtubs can vary widely depending on the brand, style, and features. Basic models are more budget-friendly, while designer or premium fixtures come at a higher price point.
  • Cabinetry and countertops: Custom or semi-custom cabinetry made from materials like laminate, wood, or MDF will have different price points. Similarly, countertop materials like laminate, granite, quartz, or marble vary in cost based on quality and brand.
  • Tiles: Tile selection for walls, floors, and shower surrounds can range from basic subway tiles to intricate mosaic patterns or natural stone. The price per square foot will depend on the type, size, and design of the tiles chosen.

Bathroom Renovation, DUMBO Brooklyn, NYC

In New York City with standard bathrooms averaging 35-40 sqf the below costs can be considered:

  • Basic bathroom renovation: A basic bathroom renovation in NYC, which may include cosmetic updates like replacing fixtures, tiles, and paint, can cost anywhere from $15,000 to $18,000. This cost may increase if plumbing or electrical work is required.
  • Mid-Range bathroom renovation: A mid-range renovation, which involves updating fixtures, cabinets, countertops, and flooring with moderately priced materials, typically ranges from $35,000 to $45,000. This budget allows for some customization and higher-quality finishes.
  • High-End bathroom renovation: For a high-end bathroom renovation with luxury fixtures, custom cabinetry, premium tiles, and high-quality materials, the cost can exceed $50,000 and go up to $80,000 or more.

Whether you're on a budget-friendly mission or ready to splurge on luxury, let your new-found knowledge of New York bathroom renovation guide your decisions. After all, in the city where dreams are made, why shouldn't your bathroom be one of them?
