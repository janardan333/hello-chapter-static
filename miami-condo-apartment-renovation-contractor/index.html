Condo renovation in Miami: What’s Involved

Renovating a condo in Miami involves detailed planning, coordination, and navigating HOA condo rules. Chapter fully manages your condo renovation, ensuring each step aligns perfectly with your vision, budget, and building's specific requirements.

Here are the key steps involved in a Miami condo renovation:

Here are the key steps
involved in a Miami condo renovation:

Planning & design

We partner with you to clearly define renovation goals. Our complimentary design services ensure your condo’s layout maximizes available space and your budget, turning your ideas into an actionable renovation plan.

Architecture & building approval

Our architect creates comprehensive plans tailored to meet your municipality codes and your condo association's rules. Chapter fully manages approval processes, acquiring necessary permits and condo board clearances to avoid potential delays.

Demolition & site protection

We meticulously safeguard common spaces and adjacent units throughout demolition. Outdated walls, flooring, and fixtures are carefully removed, minimizing disturbance and ensuring your building stays clean and undamaged.

Bathroom renovation

Our bathroom renovations optimize your space for comfort and style. New fixtures, custom tile work, waterproofing, and efficient layouts significantly enhance the aesthetic appeal and functionality of your bathrooms.

Kitchen renovation

We revitalize your kitchen with a balance of beauty and practicality. Updated cabinetry, premium appliances, elegant countertops, and stylish backsplashes create an attractive, efficient cooking environment tailored to your lifestyle.

Electrical & plumbing upgrades

We upgrade plumbing and electrical systems to modern standards, ensuring compliance with your municipality codes. New installations of lines, fixtures, and appliances improve functionality, safety, and reliability in your renovated condo.

Air conditioning & ventilation systems

Our team evaluates and updates your condo’s air conditioning and ventilation systems, ensuring maximum comfort, optimal air quality, and energy efficiency specifically designed for Miami’s climate conditions.

Custom carpentry & millwork

Expert carpenters craft and install custom elements such as framing, drywall modifications, built-in cabinetry, doors, windows, trim, and detailed moldings. Each installation enhances your condo’s structural integrity and visual appeal.

Quality assurance & final inspections

Upon completing construction, we finalize permits and conduct thorough quality inspections. We confirm all renovations meet your local municipality and condo building standards, guaranteeing a final result that meets your expectations.

Condo renovations in Miami typically range from $150 to $350 per square foot, depending on the project's complexity, materials selected, and specific building requirements.

Yes, typical restrictions specific to Miami condos include:

  • Permitted work hours: Usually restricted to weekdays, between 9 AM and 4 PM.
  • Noise regulations: Strictly enforced to minimize disturbances to other residents.
  • Material requirements: Certain materials may be restricted due to building standards or Miami’s hurricane preparedness requirements.
  • Elevator use: Requires scheduling and protective measures to transport materials and debris without causing damage.

Always verify details directly with your condo management or HOA.

A full condo renovation in Miami generally takes between 3 to 8 months. The specific timeline can vary depending on project scope, customization, and building regulations.

The permit approval process in Miami can vary based on the project's complexity and the specific municipality. For smaller projects, permits might be issued within 6-12 weeks. However, more extensive renovations often require 3-6 months timeframe. It's essential to consult with local authorities or a professional familiar with Miami's permitting process to get an accurate estimate for your specific project.

Permits are generally required for:

  • Structural changes: Moving or removing walls.
  • Mechanical, electrical, and plumbing work:Installing or modifying systems.
  • Window and door replacements:Especially if altering sizes or locations.
  • Flooring installations: Particularly in multi-unit buildings where soundproofing is a concern.
  • Kitchen and bathroom remodels: Involving plumbing or electrical changes. Miami-Dade County

Always check with your local building department and condo association to determine specific permit requirements.

Yes, most condo renovations in Miami require architectural plans. For projects involving structural changes, mechanical, electrical, or plumbing work, plans must be prepared and signed by a licensed architect or engineer.
