Bathroom renovations: what’s involved

There are many parts to consider for a bathroom remodel. Chapter assumes responsibility for managing your entire project, so that every aspect aligns with your goals, budget, and the current state of your bathroom.

Here's a broad outline of the usual components involved in a bathroom renovation:

Here's a broad outline of the usual components involved in a bathroom renovation:

Planning & design

Define your goals and priorities to create a remodel plan that includes every detail of your bathroom remodel project. Work with a designer to envision the ideal bathroom layout and aesthetics.

Layout changes

Inspect existing conditions to identify possible bathroom layouts. Significant layout changes, such as relocating plumbing lines and walls, may involve full architectural plans or structural carpentry work.

Demolition

Protect surfaces and common areas. Remove existing plumbing fixtures, tiles, accessories, water and waste line rough-ins. Address structural issues that may be uncovered during demolition.

Plumbing & electrical

Upgrade or replace plumbing fixtures, including replace pipes to improve efficiency and meet current building codes. Update or relocate electrical outlets and switches. Install new lighting fixtures.

Floor & wall coverings

Prepare surfaces, ensure proper waterproofing, and install new tiles with precision details, including grout, sealant, and trim. Attention to detail is crucial for a durable and visually cohesive result.

Cabinetry & storage

Design and install cabinets, vanities, and storage solutions to enhance organization and maximize available space. Select from custom or pre-made options based on your budget, timeline, and preferences.

Fixtures & accessories

Installation or replacement of bathroom fixtures, including sinks, faucets, toilets, bathtubs, and showers, to meet design standards and improve functionality.

Appliances

Purchase and install new appliances, such as a washer and dryer, ventilation, heated towel rack, and electric radiant floor heating. These are the details to enhance functionality and comfort.

Quality assurance & inspection

Close out permits and necessary signoffs. Conduct a final inspection and quality assurance check to guarantee design adherence and code compliance.

FAQs

A bathroom renovation typically costs between $35,000 and $50,000, although the price may vary based on certain factors, including project scope, finishes, and structural changes.

A bathroom renovation typically takes 4-6 weeks, but the timeline may vary based on certain factors, including project scope and structural changes.

Most bathroom renovations require plumbing permits. Structural and layout changes may require full architectural plans and permits. You can avoid the permit process if you're only replacing tiles and accessories.

It is possible to make layout changes to your bathroom, but the feasibility depends on whether you live in a building or a house. In buildings, there are often more limitations due to structural considerations and building regulations. We recommend consulting with us to assess your bathroom renovation project.

In many cases, hiring an architect may not be necessary for bathroom renovations. Most projects can be executed with plumbing permits instead of full architectural plans. With Chapter, you have the advantage of access to a contractor, designer, and architect, all in one place. The result is a streamlined, efficient bathroom remodel plus a great renovation experience.
