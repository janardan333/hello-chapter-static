How much does it cost to renovate a townhouse in Brooklyn?

Zach Barnes-Corby
Head of Sales

Considering remodeling a Brooklyn townhouse? Most likely, you're curious about the upcoming financial commitment. From the charming brownstones of Park Slope to the emerging neighborhoods of Crown Heights, Brooklyn townhouse renovations present unique opportunities that directly impact your budget.

The quick response? In 2025, the average cost of renovating a Brooklyn townhouse is $350 to $550 per square foot. There's a lot more to the story, though, and knowing the specifics could save you tens of thousands of dollars.

Chapter Estimation Team

Understanding Brooklyn townhouse renovation costs

Average cost by project scope

  • Renovations on a tight budget: $75,000 to $150,000

Maintaining current layouts; minor kitchen or bathroom improvements; cosmetic updates (painting, refinishing flooring); and simple fixture replacements

  • Mid-range projects: $150,000 - $300,000

Modern fixtures and appliances; opening up interior walls; updating mechanical systems; complete kitchen or bathroom renovations.

  • High-end luxury renovations: $300,000 - $1,000,000+

Complete mechanical system replacement; premium materials and custom millwork; historical restoration work; gut renovations with structural modifications.

Room-by-room cost breakdown

Kitchen renovations

Range: $25,000 - $100,000+

Brooklyn townhouse kitchens often require creative solutions for narrow layouts:

  • Basic refresh: $25,000 - $40,000
  • Mid-range remodel: $40,000 - $70,000
  • High-end custom kitchen: $70,000 - $150,000+

Key cost factors:

  • Custom cabinetry for awkward layouts
  • Moving plumbing/gas lines
  • Structural work for open concepts
  • High-end appliances

Kitchen Renovation, Brooklyn, NY

Bathroom renovations

Range: $20,000 - $60,000 per bathroom

Brooklyn townhouse kitchens often require creative solutions for narrow layouts:

  • Powder room update: $10,000 - $20,000
  • Full bathroom renovation: $30,000 - $40,000
  • Master suite luxury bathroom: $40,000 - $60,000+

Structural and systems

Electrical system upgrade

  • Full townhouse rewiring: $12,000 - $30,000
  • Panel upgrade: $3,000 - $7,500
  • Adding outlets/lighting: $150 - $300 per outlet

Plumbing overhaul

  • Full system replacement: $15,000 - $25,000
  • Bathroom relocations: $5,000 - $15,000 per move
  • Kitchen plumbing relocation: $3,000 - $8,000

HVAC installation

  • entral air system: $15,000 - $25,000
  • Ductless mini-splits: $8,000 - $15,000
  • Heating system upgrade: $10,000 - $20,000

Factors that impact your Brooklyn townhouse renovation cost

1. Historic considerations

Brooklyn's historic townhouses often require:

  • Landmark compliance: Additional $10,000 - $25,000 in fees and specialized materials
  • Historic restoration: 25-50% premium for specialized craftsmen
  • Original feature preservation: $5,000 - $20,000 per room for plaster/millwork restoration

2. Structural challenges

Many Brooklyn townhouses built in the early 1900s present:

  • Foundation issues: $15,000 - $50,000
  • Structural beam replacement: $5,000 - $15,000 per beam
  • Floor leveling: $3,000 - $8,000 per floor

3. Configuration changes

  • Wall removal: $1,000 - $5,000 per wall
  • Staircase relocation: $15,000 - $35,000
  • Floor plan reconfiguration: 15-25% of total renovation cost

4. Brooklyn-specific factors

  • Labor costs: 20-30% higher than national average
  • Material delivery: Additional $2,000 - $5,000 for city logistics
  • Permit fees: $5,000 - $15,000 depending on scope
  • Parking permits for contractors: $200 - $500 per month

Red flags and costly mistakes to avoid

Common expensive errors

  • Skipping permits: Can cost 2-3x more to fix later
  • Cutting corners on waterproofing: Leads to extensive damage
  • Ignoring structural issues: Compounds over time
  • Over-leveraging: Spending more than neighborhood comps support

Conclusion

Renovating a Brooklyn townhouse is a significant investment that can transform your living space and add substantial value to your property. With costs ranging from $350-550 per square foot for comprehensive renovations, proper planning and budgeting are essential for success.

Remember, while the upfront costs may seem substantial, a well-executed townhouse renovation in Brooklyn can provide decades of enjoyment and strong returns on your investment. Take time to plan thoroughly, budget conservatively, and work with professionals who understand the unique challenges of renovating in New York City.
